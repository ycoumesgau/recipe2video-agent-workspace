# Suno Prompt — Bibimbap coréen

## Status

- Recipe: Bibimbap coréen
- Goal: generate a long song that can be used as a full version, then edited into a shorter version for the vertical video.
- Target model: Suno 5.5 in Advanced / Custom Mode.
- Video use: TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels.

## Intent

The song should mirror the video arc: an immediate golden-yolk hook, rice steam, sesame-chili gloss, beef sizzle, colorful radial bowl assembly, and a proud Licorn kitchen ending. It should feel bright, crisp, savory-sweet, and easy to cut into a short vertical edit.

## Session notes (voice, length, hook)

**Voice**

- Select a bright, close-mic pop vocal persona if available.
- Do not ask Suno to imitate a real singer, an existing artist, or any protected voice.

**Structure**

- Generate the long version first, aiming for 2 to 3 minutes.
- Make the chorus reusable for a 45 to 90 second video edit.
- Keep the lyrics image-driven, not procedural.

Do not include genre, production, instrument, mix, or vocal-style instructions. Do not write a recipe tutorial, quantities, numbered steps, brand names, software names, or social media references.

Mood: bright, glossy, playful, savory-sweet, colorful, food-video ASMR energy, but not childish.

Lyric style: short singable lines, clipped phrasing, catchy repetitions, call-and-response energy, simple hooks, occasional parenthetical echoes like "(bibimbap)". Keep the chorus reusable for a 45-90 second video edit.

Story and imagery to weave in naturally:
- golden yolk breaking and running into white rice
- sesame shine, chili gloss, tiny white seeds

- beef strips sizzling in a dark soy-sesame glaze

Yolk, rice cloud, sesame sparkle, chili shine, the first glossy hook.

## Short Version To Extract Later

For a 45-90 second vertical edit, use a very short intro or immediate Verse 1 line over the yolk-break hook, keep the pre-chorus for the beef sizzle and vegetable color build, land the full chorus on radial assembly, sauce pour, yolk break payoff, and final hero bowl. If the bridge has a softer spoon-dip image, use 2-4 seconds of it before the final chorus over the satisfied Licorn ending.
